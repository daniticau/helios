--- SEGMENT 00_hook ---
# 0:00–0:15 hook (~40 words)

Installing home solar in California has a six to ten year payback. But the math behind that number lives in five different places — your utility's time-of-use plan, NEM 3.0 export rates, installer quotes, financing APRs, system sizing — and none of them talk to each other. So people don't install.

--- SEGMENT 01_mode_a ---
# 0:15–0:45 Mode A + Orthogonal ticker (~75 words)

Meet Helios. You enter a San Diego address. You tap go. Watch what happens.

Tariff lookup. Weather forecast. Installer pricing. Financing rates. Rebate news. Property value. Demographics. Installer reviews. Carbon pricing. ZenPower permits.

Ten APIs. Firing in parallel. Real endpoints, real latencies, all streaming back in under twenty seconds.

Result: break even in six point eight years. Twenty-five-year net present value: positive forty-eight thousand two hundred dollars. That's eleven percent of your home's value. Twelve point four tons of carbon avoided — worth twenty-one hundred dollars at the current social cost of carbon.

--- SEGMENT 02_orthogonal_pitch ---
# 0:45–1:00 Orthogonal value prop (~45 words)

Building this integration layer against ten paid APIs the normal way is a week of signups, API key rotation, and billing dashboards. Property data vendors, people data, review scrapers, a carbon price feed, financing lookups. We wrote one SDK integration instead of ten. Orthogonal: metered, pay-per-use, zero keys to manage. This product genuinely could not exist without it.

--- SEGMENT 03_share_extension ---
# 1:00–1:30 share extension (~70 words)

Quit the app. Open a real PG&E bill in Safari. Tap share. Helios appears in the share sheet — alongside Messages, alongside Mail. Tap it.

The app opens. The bill is parsed automatically. Address pre-filled. Monthly kilowatt-hours detected. ROI recomputes in twenty seconds — and the Orthogonal ticker runs again, ten fresh calls, fresh latencies.

This is an actual iOS share extension. Not a mock. Not a screenshot. Real intent handling.

--- SEGMENT 04_mode_b ---
# 1:30–2:30 Mode B (~150 words)

Now switch profiles. Tijuana hills, eight kilowatt solar array, Powerwall battery, already installed.

Mode B is live dashboard mode. The arbitrage engine evaluates six possible battery actions every minute, against live CAISO wholesale prices, retail time-of-use rates, and a twenty-four-hour solar forecast.

Current action: charge the battery. Peak export window opens in forty-two minutes. Expected earnings over the next four hours: eighteen dollars and forty cents.

Here's the home-screen widget. Sell now, plus one dollar forty-two per hour. Green dot. Battery state of charge: eighty-seven percent.

A push notification fires ten minutes before any forecasted peak window with an export rate above eighty cents per kilowatt-hour. Tap the notification, deep-link into the hourly plan.

Forecast chart shows the next twenty-four hours — retail and export rates overlaid with predicted solar production. The exact minute-by-minute recommendation is right there.
